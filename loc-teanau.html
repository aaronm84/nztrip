Day 10 · The Quiet Lake

Te Anau

The gateway town to Fiordland, silent in late autumn — one good dinner and a long sleep.

← The Atlas

After the cruise lets us off mid-morning and the waterfall stops on the drive out, Te Anau is deliberately nothing: a lakefront walk, an early dinner, sleep. Stock provisions here for tomorrow’s long drive — the next real town is hours away.

Where we eat

The table playbook (every restaurant, every time): say coeliac, not gluten free · flag the shrimp, mango & date allergies — shrimp paste hides in Korean & Thai sauces, kimchi and curry pastes; mango sneaks into Asian salads and desserts; dates hide in tagines, sticky desserts and "energy" anything · her plate: fish, chicken or plants over red meat, and cow dairy only in moderation — goat, sheep & hard aged cheeses are fine. NZ king salmon is the anniversary-grade anti-inflammatory order.

The Redcliff Restaurant & Bar

Mokonui St · Fiordland fine-rustic · mains ~NZ$38–52

The town’s standout by a mile — a cosy cottage doing Southland lamb, wild venison and salmon, with staff repeatedly praised by coeliac diners for careful handling. Order her the salmon; the venison is the house’s pride if you want it.

The Fat Duck

Lakefront · bistro · ~NZ$28–44

The casual alternative — GF swaps offered across much of the menu (bao becomes a lettuce cup or crepe). Twice-cooked pork belly is the crowd order; she takes the lamb salad or chowder after a GF check.
