Day 11 · The Mountain

Aoraki / Mt Cook

New Zealand’s highest mountain, its flattest great walk, and the darkest sky either of us will ever stand under.

← The Atlas

The drive in via Twizel crosses the plains that played the Pelennor Fields, then dead-ends at a village of maybe 250 people beneath a 12,218-foot mountain. Freezing, cloudless nights are the whole point.

Where we sleep

Aoraki Court Motel

Village centre · ~NZ$300–450/night

The village’s best rooms: heated floors, full kitchens, spa-bath options, mountain views, and trailheads on foot. Confirmed warm by winter guests — and the kitchen means one fully-controlled GF dinner. Book this the same week as the cruise; there is almost no plan B here.

Where we eat

The table playbook (every restaurant, every time): say coeliac, not gluten free · flag the shrimp, mango & date allergies — shrimp paste hides in Korean & Thai sauces, kimchi and curry pastes; mango sneaks into Asian salads and desserts; dates hide in tagines, sticky desserts and "energy" anything · her plate: fish, chicken or plants over red meat, and cow dairy only in moderation — goat, sheep & hard aged cheeses are fine. NZ king salmon is the anniversary-grade anti-inflammatory order.

The Old Mountaineers’ Café

Village · café-restaurant · ~NZ$24–38

The independent café with the glacier view — GF options marked, honest mountain food, and walls of climbing history. Lunch or early dinner.
