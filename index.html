Ten years deserves a far country.

We land in Auckland at the end of April, when the poplars of Central Otago turn to gold and the first snow settles on the Southern Alps. Twelve days, two islands, and the exact country where the films were made — from the lanterns of the Shire to the ash fields of Mordor, then south across the water to fjords, wine, and the darkest night sky either of us will ever stand under.

The whole trip is built cold on purpose: highs in the fifties, mornings near freezing, and not one hour anywhere near a hot afternoon. Layers, boots, and merino. Exactly right.

Days 1–2 · North Island

Auckland & the Shire

Days 3–4 · North Island

Wellington

Fly south between the mountains. Wine caves, the Glenorchy road, the Routeburn.
