Days 6–8 · The Southern Base

Queenstown

Three nights under the Remarkables — wine country east, Lothlórien north-west, and the flex day in the middle.

The adventure capital wears autumn beautifully — gold poplars, snow arriving on the tops, and the crowds gone home. It is also the easiest GF town on the South Island.

Where we sleep

The Rees Hotel

Lakeside, 5 min from town · ~NZ$400–700/night

The value-luxury pick: every room faces the lake and the Remarkables, there’s a serious wine cellar, and a free shuttle into town. Lakeview Hotel Rooms are the sweet spot; Executive Apartments add a full kitchen.

SiteMap

Eichardt’s Private Hotel — the splurge

Marine Parade, in town · ~NZ$1,500–2,500+/night

Lakefront in the absolute centre, a handful of suites, champagne and canapés included, Michelin Stay recognition. One night here mid-stay is the anniversary flex if the budget smiles.

SiteMap

Azur Lodge — the secluded alternative

Above the lake · villas · ~NZ$1,200–1,900/night incl. breakfast

Nine private villas with wall-of-glass lake views and spa baths. If the pick is romance-by-isolation rather than town-at-the-door, this is it.

Where we eat

The table playbook (every restaurant, every time): say coeliac, not gluten free · flag the shrimp, mango & date allergies — shrimp paste hides in Korean & Thai sauces, kimchi and curry pastes; mango sneaks into Asian salads and desserts; dates hide in tagines, sticky desserts and "energy" anything · her plate: fish, chicken or plants over red meat, and cow dairy only in moderation — goat, sheep & hard aged cheeses are fine. NZ king salmon is the anniversary-grade anti-inflammatory order.

Erik’s Fish & Chips

Earl St · dedicated GF fryer · ~NZ$15–25

Famous for a completely separate coeliac fryer — GF battered fish and chips that are actually safe. The definitive her-order in town: blue cod, no dairy, all anti-inflammatory.
