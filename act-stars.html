What it is

The Aoraki Mackenzie International Dark Sky Reserve is one of the largest and darkest on earth. On a clear, freezing late-autumn night: the Milky Way core, the Magellanic Clouds, and the Southern Cross — none of which exist in a Tennessee sky.

For the two of us

Guided sessions (telescopes, laser tours) run from the Hermitage in the village; the free version is stepping outside Aoraki Court at midnight in every layer we own. Cold tolerance is a superpower here — the best viewing nights are the coldest ones. Have this as the plan and the flex day as its weather backup.

What people say

Reviewers’ one consistent note: dress for far colder than feels reasonable, and give eyes 20 minutes to adjust. Clear-night reviews are life-list stuff.
