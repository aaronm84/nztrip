The walk

The opening stretch of one of the most famous walks on earth: red beech forest, a gorge where the Route Burn runs unreasonably blue, swing bridges, and a final opening onto golden grass flats ringed by snowline peaks. We walk in, eat Mrs Woolly’s picnic at the Flats, and walk out.

What walkers say

Even walkers who’ve done the full Great Walk call this first section the prettiest easy kilometre-for-kilometre. Late autumn reviewers mention having it nearly to themselves after the hut season closes April 30 — facilities are reduced, the track remains.

Our notes

The road past Glenorchy to the shelter includes unsealed stretches — fine in the rental, just slow. Sandflies own the car park; repellent before boots.
