Where we sleep

Discovery Lodge — chalet

SH47, park edge · ~NZ$250–350/night

Book a chalet, not a hut — private bathroom, heating, and a straight-on view of the volcanoes from bed. The grand old Chateau in the village is closed, which makes this the view in town.

Where we eat

The table playbook (every restaurant, every time): say coeliac, not gluten free · flag the shrimp, mango & date allergies — shrimp paste hides in Korean & Thai sauces, kimchi and curry pastes; mango sneaks into Asian salads and desserts; dates hide in tagines, sticky desserts and "energy" anything · her plate: fish, chicken or plants over red meat, and cow dairy only in moderation — goat, sheep & hard aged cheeses are fine. NZ king salmon is the anniversary-grade anti-inflammatory order.

Station Café — National Park Village

Findlay St · café-bistro · mains ~NZ$20–35

A converted railway station doing honest café food; GF options exist but this is a small-town kitchen — say coeliac clearly and keep orders simple (steak, fish, salads).
