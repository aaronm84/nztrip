Days 4–5 · The Capital

Wellington

The best eating city in the country, the harbour, the wind, and the workshop where Middle-earth was built.

← The Atlas

Compact, walkable, and genuinely great at gluten free — Wellington has more coeliac-safe kitchens per block than anywhere else on this trip. Cuba Street is the food spine; the waterfront is the walk.

Where we sleep

The Intrepid Hotel

Ghuznee St, edge of Cuba · boutique · ~NZ$300–450/night

An art deco boutique with a lobby honesty bar and the city’s best eating at the door. Ask for an upper floor away from weekend bar noise. Rooms are snug and beautiful rather than big.

QT Wellington — alternative

Waterfront, by Te Papa · ~NZ$280–420/night

If art-hotel maximalism and a waterfront position appeal more than Cuba Street, the QT is the character alternative — bigger rooms, stranger art.

Where we eat

The table playbook (every restaurant, every time): say coeliac, not gluten free · flag the shrimp, mango & date allergies — shrimp paste hides in Korean & Thai sauces, kimchi and curry pastes; mango sneaks into Asian salads and desserts; dates hide in tagines, sticky desserts and "energy" anything · her plate: fish, chicken or plants over red meat, and cow dairy only in moderation — goat, sheep & hard aged cheeses are fine. NZ king salmon is the anniversary-grade anti-inflammatory order.

Miss Kangsta

Jessie St · Korean · dedicated GF · ~NZ$25–38

A fully gluten-free Korean kitchen — dumplings, bibimbap, fried chicken, all coeliac-safe. THE allergy note of the trip: Korean cooking leans on shrimp paste (kimchi, some sauces) — name the shrimp allergy explicitly and they’ll steer you.

Charley Noble

Post Office Square · grill & seafood · mains ~NZ$36–55

Coeliac NZ–accredited with a famously deep GF menu — the special-night pick. Wood-grilled fish for her; the raw bar has oysters, but watch the crustacean side of the menu for cross-over and re-flag the shrimp allergy.

Karaka Café

Waterfront · Māori kai · ~NZ$20–34

Traditional flavours — hāngī plates, kaimoana — with GF marked and staff who know what coeliac means. A lunch spot with the harbour in front of you.

Santeria — GF pizza night

Coeliac-accredited pizza with two GF base options and allergen-labelled toppings, steps from The Intrepid. Goat cheese on hers keeps the dairy in bounds.
