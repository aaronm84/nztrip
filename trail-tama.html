The walk

The same start as Taranaki Falls, then onward across open alpine tussock into the saddle between Ruapehu and Ngauruhoe, where two old explosion craters hold cold blue lakes. The Lower Tama lookout is the natural turnaround; the Upper adds a steep, loose scramble.

What walkers say

The consensus: the first lookout carries most of the reward, and the out-and-back format makes it honest — turn around whenever it stops being fun. Reviewers flag the exposed saddle wind and how fast weather moves here.

Our notes

This is the hike most likely to be swapped if early snow arrives — check DOC conditions the night before. No water or shelter past the falls; carry everything.
